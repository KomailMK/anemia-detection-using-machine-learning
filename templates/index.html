Understanding Anemia

What is Anemia?

Anemia is a condition characterized by a lack of healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to the body's tissues, causing fatigue and weakness.

How it Occurs

Anemia can develop due to blood loss, decreased or faulty red blood cell production, or destruction of red blood cells.

Common Symptoms

Symptoms include fatigue, weakness, pale skin, irregular heartbeat, shortness of breath, dizziness, and cold hands and feet.
